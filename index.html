Top 15 Places to visit in Gujrat

Gujarat, located on the western coast of India, is a culturally rich and diverse state known for its vibrant heritage and stunning landscapes. One of its most iconic attractions is the Statue of Unity, the world's tallest statue, dedicated to Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel.

Gir National Park is home to the rare Asiatic lions, making it a must-visit for wildlife lovers. The Rann of Kutch, famous for its vast white salt desert, becomes magical during the Rann Utsav with cultural festivities. History enthusiasts can explore Dwarka, one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites, and the ancient ruins of Lothal, one of the prominent cities of the Indus Valley Civilization.

Here is the list of most visited places in Gujrat.

1. The Statue of Unity

The Statue of Unity is the tallest statue in the world, standing at 182 meters (597 feet). It is located near Kevadia in the Narmada district of Gujarat, India. This majestic monument is dedicated to Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, India’s first Deputy Prime Minister and a key figure in uniting the nation after independence.

The statue overlooks the Sardar Sarovar Dam on the Narmada River, surrounded by beautiful hills and lush landscapes. Besides the statue itself, the site features an observation deck with panoramic views, a museum, audio-visual gallery, valley of flowers, and boat rides.

Inaugurated on October 31, 2018, it has since become one of India’s most visited tourist attractions and a symbol of national pride and unity.

2. Gir National Park

Gir National Park, located in the Junagadh district of Gujarat, is the only natural habitat of the endangered Asiatic lion. Spread across over 1,400 square kilometers, the park is a dry deciduous forest with rich biodiversity. Apart from lions, it is home to leopards, hyenas, jackals, antelopes, and over 300 species of birds.

The park was established in 1965 to conserve the lion population, which has since steadily grown. Tourists can enjoy jeep safaris to explore the forest and witness wildlife in their natural environment. The best time to visit is December to March, while the park remains closed from June to October during the monsoon.

3. Somnath Temple

Somnath Temple, located in Prabhas Patan near Veraval in Gujarat, is one of the most revered and ancient Hindu temples in India. It is the first of the 12 Jyotirlingas, dedicated to Lord Shiva, and holds immense religious significance. The temple has a history of destruction and reconstruction, symbolizing eternal resilience and faith.

Its present structure, built in Chalukya style in 1951, stands majestically on the Arabian Sea coast, offering a breathtaking view. Pilgrims and tourists visit year-round for its spiritual aura, beautiful architecture, and evening light shows. Somnath’s legacy continues as a beacon of India's rich cultural and spiritual heritage.

4. Dwarka Temple

Dwarka Temple, also known as the Dwarkadhish Temple, is a sacred Hindu shrine located in Dwarka, Gujarat. Dedicated to Lord Krishna, it is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites and one of the seven most ancient religious cities (Sapta Puri) in India. The temple's grand five-storied structure, built on 72 pillars, reflects intricate Chalukya-style architecture and stands proudly near the Gomti River.

According to legend, Dwarka was the capital of Lord Krishna’s kingdom. Pilgrims flock here to seek blessings and experience the spiritual energy. The temple also hosts vibrant festivals, especially Janmashtami, drawing thousands of devotees. It remains a timeless symbol of devotion, history, and divine legacy.

5. Ahmedabad City

Ahmedabad, the largest city in Gujarat, is a vibrant blend of history, culture, and modern development. Situated on the banks of the Sabarmati River, it is famous for the Sabarmati Ashram, once home to Mahatma Gandhi, and its role in India’s freedom movement. The city is known for its textile industry, earning it the nickname "Manchester of India."

Ahmedabad boasts stunning architecture, from the Adalaj Stepwell to the Sidi Saiyyed Mosque. Its old city area is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, rich in heritage and traditions. Festivals like Uttarayan and Navratri are celebrated with great enthusiasm. Today, Ahmedabad is also a growing hub for education, business, and technology.

6. Modhera Sun Temple

The Modhera Sun Temple, located in Mehsana district of Gujarat, is a stunning example of 11th-century Solanki architecture dedicated to Surya, the Sun God. Built by King Bhima I of the Chaulukya dynasty in 1026 AD, the temple is renowned for its precise alignment with the solar equinox. At sunrise, the first rays of the sun illuminate the sanctum.

The temple complex features a richly carved Sabha Mandap, Guda Mandap, and the Surya Kund, a stepped water tank. Though the idol is missing today, the temple remains a masterpiece of devotion and architectural brilliance. It is a protected monument and a popular tourist attraction, especially during the Modhera Dance Festival.

7. Kutch White Desert

Rann of Kutch, often called the White Desert, is a vast salt marsh located in the Thar Desert of Gujarat’s Kutch district. Spanning over 7,500 square kilometers, its gleaming white expanse transforms under sunlight into a shimmering landscape.

The Rann Utsav festival, held annually from November to February, showcases local culture with folk music, dance performances, handicrafts, and camel rides. Wildlife—including flamingos, wild asses, and desert foxes—adds to its allure. Nights reveal spectacular starry skies, complemented by traditional tents, magical camping experiences, and cultural experiences. The unique ecosystem and breathtaking vistas make it a must-visit destination for photographers, nature lovers, and cultural enthusiasts exploring western India’s desert heart.

8. Rani Stepwell(Vav)

Rani ki Vav, located in Patan, Gujarat, India, is an exquisite seven-story stepwell built in the 11th century by Queen Udayamati in memory of her husband, King Bhima I. Designed to collect and store rainwater, this architectural marvel combines functionality with artistry.

Its walls, pillars, and corridors are adorned with more than 500 principal sculptures and over a thousand minor ones depicting Hindu deities and decorative motifs. The stepwell’s flights of steps descend to the water level, creating ambience. Discovered in the mid-20th century and restored, Rani ki Vav was designated a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2014. Today, it stands as a testament to India’s ingenuity, spirituality, and craftsmanship.

9. Saputara Hills

Saputara, located at an altitude of 900 meters, offers stunning views of lush green valleys, rolling hills, and forests. The town's centerpiece is Saputara Lake, surrounded by gardens, a boathouse, and craft shops. Visitors can enjoy viewpoints like Sunset Point and Sunrise Point, plus the Gira Waterfalls.

Activities include boating, trekking along forest trails, and a ropeway linking to nearby peaks. The Saputara Tribal Museum showcases rich tribal heritage, while local markets add to the charm. With pleasant weather year-round, especially from October to March, it's a perfect getaway for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

10. Vadodara City

Vadodara, historically known as Baroda, is a vibrant city in the Indian state of Gujarat. Located on the banks of the Vishwamitri River, it serves as the cultural and educational capital of the state. Vadodara is famous for the majestic Laxmi Vilas Palace, of the largest residences in the world, built in Indo-Saracenic style.

The city hosts the annual Navratri festival, drawing visitors for its Garba dance. Its cultural scene includes museums, art galleries, and performing arts venues. Renowned educational institutions such as the Maharaja Sayajirao University contribute to its reputation. Green spaces like Sayaji Baug offer tranquil retreats. A hub for commerce and industry, Vadodara balances tradition and modernity.

11. Shree Ambaji Temple

Ambaji Temple, nestled on the Gabbar hill in the Banaskantha district of Gujarat, is one of India’s revered Shakti Peethas. Dedicated to Goddess Amba, this ancient temple attracts millions of pilgrims yearly. Its current marble structure, built in the nineteenth century, reflects traditional Hindu architectural styles, featuring ornate pillars and a gold‑plated roof. According to legend, the heart of Sati fell here, endowing the site with profound spiritual significance.

The temple complex includes multiple shrines, a sacred kund (pond), and the famous Trinetra (three‑eyed) shrine. Each Navratri, vibrant fairs, devotional music, and traditional Garba dances enliven the hilltop. Ambaji’s serene mountain setting and devotional atmosphere make it a premier pilgrimage destination.

12. Pavagadh Temple

Pavagadh Temple, also known as the Kalika Mata Temple, is a revered Hindu shrine perched at an altitude of approximately 800 meters on Pavagadh Hill in Gujarat’s Panchmahal district. Dedicated to Goddess Kali, it dates back to the 10th century, with architectural elements reflecting both Maratha and Chandela influences.

Pilgrims ascend the hill via a stone staircase of 300 steps or the ropeway, enjoying panoramic views of the UNESCO World Heritage site spanning Pavagadh and Champaner. The temple complex features intricate stone carvings, a sacred pond, and the adjacent Bhadrakali Temple. Annual festivals such as Navratri and the Makar Sankranti fair draw thousands seeking blessings in this serene hilltop setting.

13. Girnar mountain

Girnar Hills, a majestic mountain range rising to about 1,000 meters near Junagadh in Gujarat, is one of India’s oldest sacred sites. The complex of peaks—Girnar Taleti, Neminath, and Ambaji—hosts ancient temples and shrines dating back over a thousand years. Pilgrims undertake the rigorous climb of around 10,000 stone steps to reach the Jain temples of Neminath, dedicated to the 22nd Tirthankara, and the Dattatreya shrine, honoring a manifestation of the Hindu Trinity.

The diverse flora and fauna, cool climate, and panoramic views of the surrounding plains and Gir Forest add to its allure. Festivals like Maha Shivaratri draw thousands of devotees, making Girnar both a spiritual and natural treasure.

14. Sivrajpur Beach

Sivrajpur Beach is a pristine coastal stretch located in the village of Vidyanagar near Dwarka in Gujarat. This tranquil beach, framed by rocky outcrops and swaying palm trees, features soft golden sand and crystal-clear turquoise waters. Renowned for its vibrant coral reefs, it is a popular spot for snorkeling and scuba diving, offering glimpses of colorful marine life.

Visitors can enjoy peaceful sunbathing, beach volleyball, and leisurely strolls along the shoreline. The beach offers stunning sunrise views over the Arabian Sea, creating a serene ambiance. Local vendors sell fresh seafood and refreshments. The best time to visit is between October and March, when the weather is pleasant and calm seas.

15. Akshardham Temple

BAPS Swaminarayan Akshardham Temple in Ahmedabad is a stunning modern shrine dedicated to Bhagwan Swaminarayan, inaugurated in 2002 by the Bochasanwasi Akshar Purushottam Swaminarayan Sanstha (BAPS). Crafted from pristine Italian Carrara marble and Rajasthani pink sandstone, the temple complex spans six acres on the banks of the Sabarmati River.

Its central sanctum features a beautifully carved idol of Swaminarayan, surrounded by 234 ornate pillars, 9 gilded domes, and 151 unique stone motifs depicting Hindu deities, flora, and fauna. Visitors explore themed gardens, a ceremonial stepwell, and an underground Abhishek Mandap. Daily light and sound shows narrate spiritual stories against the backdrop of illuminated fountains. Free guided tours, peaceful courtyards, and vegetarian cafés make it a serene cultural destination.
